RUBBER LINING

+

Our fabrication process begins with the procurement of high-grade mild steel plates, flanges, and related materials. These are cut, rolled, and welded to form equipment such as storage tanks, pressure vessels, and process reactors, in accordance with engineering drawings and applicable standards. All welded components undergo visual inspection, dimensional verification, and non destructive testing where required. Surface preparation is carried out using grit blasting to achieve the correct profile for rubber bonding. The fabricated structures are then cleaned and prepared for the next phase, ensuring readiness for either in-house lining or transport to site for further work.

FABRICATION

+

The rubber lining process starts with compounding raw rubber using selected fillers and bonding agents to achieve the desired physical and chemical properties. The compounded rubber is processed through a kneader, mixing mill, and calendering unit to produce sheets of specified thickness. Depending on the application, rubber sheets are applied using either cold bonding or autoclave curing methods. Cold bonding is used for on-site projects and involves priming, adhesive layering, and manual application of pre-cured sheets. Autoclave curing is used for factory-based jobs, where the entire assembly is subjected to controlled heat and pressure to cure the rubber lining. Post-lining inspection includes spark testing, adhesion checks, and thickness measurements to ensure full compliance with client and industry standards.
